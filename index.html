02 The market

The Size of the Opportunity

Before we talk numbers, it helps to see the size of the room you are playing in, and who else is in it. The short version: the market is huge, and there is a clear gap that fits you.

How big is the market
  • Software consulting is roughly a $380 billion market in 2026, and growing fast. North America is the largest slice of it.
  • The US consulting market alone is around $133 billion. Process improvement and modernization are among the strongest demand areas.
  • Agile and process certifications are a busy, paid market. Training courses routinely sell for $1,000 to $1,800 per seat, and companies buy them in bulk for whole teams.

You do not need a big share of a market this size to win. You need the right slice of it, and that is exactly what this plan targets.

Who else is in the room

It is an honest picture: there are large, well known names in this space. The good news is that none of them is you, and that is your advantage.

The giants

Accenture, Deloitte, IBM, Capgemini

Huge consulting firms that do process and modernization work. Powerful, but expensive, generic, and impossible to get personal attention from.

The frameworks

SAFe, PMI, Scrum Alliance

The big certification bodies. They own brand recognition, but they sell one rigid framework, not your specific, battle tested method.

The crowd

Independent coaches and trainers

Thousands of solo Agile and process coaches. Most have no original framework, no published body of work, and no twenty year track record.

Your gap

Intelligent Systems Press

A proven, original framework, five published books, two decades of real results, and a focused niche. That combination is rare, and it is defensible.

Where you win. You will not out spend Accenture or out brand SAFe, and you do not need to. Your edge is being the specific, credible, original expert in your niche, the one with the books and the proof. This plan is built to make that the first name people think of in your corner of a very large market.

06 Engine 2

Outbound: Going Straight to Your Buyers

Here is the quiet truth. The leaders who pay for consulting and training are not browsing Amazon for your books. So we do not wait for them to find you. We go to them.

This is the engine that drives the real money. We identify the exact people who buy what you sell, then reach them directly and personally by email. In simple terms, here is how it runs.

We define your perfect customer

We build clear "ideal customer profiles," the ICPs. For example: a VP of Engineering at a US software company with 200 to 2,000 staff. We do this for each thing you sell.

We build the target list

Using Clay and our verified data, we pull the real names, companies, and emails of people who match. Not random lists. The right people.

We set up clean email infrastructure

We add extra domains and inboxes so the outreach is reliable, lands in the inbox, and never puts your main brand domain at risk.

We send personal daily emails

A steady, daily flow of warm, relevant messages, written to start a conversation, not to spam. Tied to your content so the name is already familiar.

We hand you the conversations

When someone replies with interest, it goes to you and the team as a real, qualified lead, ready for a consulting, training, or speaking conversation.

The tools that make it run

In simple terms: Clay builds and organizes the target lists automatically. Enrichment tools like Prospeo find and verify the right contact details, whichever option is most accurate and cost effective. Multiple domains and inboxes keep the sending healthy and your reputation safe. And our access to 100 million plus verified US B2B contacts means we are never short of the right people to reach. This last one is a real asset, and more on why it matters shortly.

07 Engine 3

Paid Ads: Smart Retargeting, Not Expensive Guesswork

We do not waste money on costly ads aimed at strangers. We advertise to people who already know you, which is far cheaper and far more powerful.

Here is the clever part. LinkedIn is where we build the audience, but running ads there is expensive. So instead, we quietly note who engages with you on LinkedIn, then reach those same people with low cost ads on Meta, Facebook and Instagram, where they relax and scroll. Same person, fraction of the cost.

Picture how it actually plays out

A CEO sees your post on LinkedIn

It is just an awareness piece, a sharp insight about a process problem. He likes it and scrolls on. No pressure, no sell. He now knows your name.

Later, he is relaxing on Facebook

While he scrolls his personal feed, your ad appears, the one that names the exact pain he is dealing with at work. It feels like it was made for him, because it was.

He books a call

The ad leads him to book a consultation directly with you, at a tiny fraction of what a cold LinkedIn ad would have cost to reach him.

Our system qualifies him first

Before that call hits your calendar, he passes through our lead qualification, so you only spend time with people who are a real fit. No tire kickers.

For the books

Amazon ads

We catch readers at the exact moment they search for process improvement, Agile, or requirements topics. Steady, reliable book sales.

Our rule

Test, learn, scale

Start lean, find what works, then put the budget there. Every dollar follows a result, never a guess.

Why this is the smart way. Cold ads to strangers are expensive and easy to ignore. Ads to people who already engaged with you are cheap and convert. We get the reach of LinkedIn without the LinkedIn ad bill, and you only pay to talk to people who already raised their hand.
